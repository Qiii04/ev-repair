Program

How learners train

A staged pathway takes learners from the classroom to the shop floor — building EV fundamentals online, rehearsing high-voltage work in virtual reality, and finishing with supervised hands-on practice.

The pathway

Four stages, one technician-ready outcome

STAGE 01

Survey & Interview

The team surveys employers and interviews learners up front to map the real skills gap and shape the curriculum around it.

STAGE 02

Online Curriculum

Self-paced online modules build the EV fundamentals learners need before they ever put on a headset or pick up a tool.

STAGE 03

VR diagnostic simulation

Learners practice high-voltage inspection and fault-finding in a simulated environment before touching a real vehicle.

STAGE 04

Hands-on shop practice

With the fundamentals and simulated reps behind them, learners move to supervised work on real vehicles in the shop.

Why it works

Safe repetition before the real high-voltage work

Traditional automotive programs rarely cover the high-voltage systems at the heart of an electric vehicle. That leaves a gap between what shops need and what new technicians know how to do safely.

By pairing a self-paced online foundation with virtual-reality rehearsal, learners can make mistakes and build muscle memory in a setting where a wrong move carries no risk — then bring that confidence to real vehicles under supervision.
